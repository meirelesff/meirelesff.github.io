Building Academic Websites Using Quarto

How I created a simple template for my academic website using Quarto and R

Teste
Author

Fernando Meireles

Published

January 23, 2023

The problem

As an academic, having an online presence is crucial for showcasing your research and findings to a wider audience. However, creating a personal website can be a daunting task, especially when it comes to choosing the right platform.

In the past, I have used Jekyll and Hugo, but they proved to be complicated and time-consuming, especially when it came to porting content. That is why I was thrilled to discover Quarto, a simple and user-friendly platform for creating academic websites.

What is Quarto?

Quarto is a modern and flexible platform that makes it easy to create a professional and polished website in a fraction of the time it would take with other platforms. In just two hours, I was able to create a website with everything I wanted, including a portfolio, a blog, and a contact page. The platform has a user-friendly interface and a drag-and-drop editor, making it easy for even non-technical users to design their website.

Advantages

One of the features I love about Quarto is its ability to handle the R programming language, which I use extensively in my research. With Quarto, I can seamlessly integrate my R code into my website, and share my findings with the world. This is a game-changer for me, as I no longer have to worry about compatibility issues between my website and my R code.

Another advantage of using Quarto is that it is incredibly easy to update my website. The platform has a built-in content management system that makes it simple to add or edit pages, change themes, and publish posts. This is a huge time-saver for me, as I can spend more time focusing on my research, and less time worrying about maintaining my website.
